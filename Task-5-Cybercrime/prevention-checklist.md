#  'Stay Safe Online' – A Campus Survival Guide to Cyber Safety

*As college students, our lives are completely digital—from ordering late-night food to paying semester fees. Here are 8 realistic, actionable steps to protect yourself and your money.*

### 💸 Protect Your Wallet (Financial & UPI Safety)
1. **The Golden UPI Rule:** You *never* need to enter your UPI PIN to receive money. Whether you are selling an old textbook or getting a refund, if your GPay, PhonePe, or Paytm asks for a PIN, money is leaving your account. Stop and cancel the transaction.
2. **Beware of the 'Collect Request':** When selling things to strangers online, watch out for payment requests disguised as deposits. Take a strict 10-second pause to read the screen before hitting "Accept" on any popup notification in your payment app.
3. **Never Google Customer Care Numbers:** If your UPI payment fails or your Swiggy/Zomato order is stuck, only use the 'Help' or 'Support' chat *inside* the official app. The top phone numbers on a Google search are often fake, set up by scammers waiting for your call.

### 📱 Protect Your Identity & Devices
4. **Don't Pay Fees on Campus Wi-Fi:** Open Wi-Fi networks in college cafes, libraries, or hostels are easy to hack. If you are paying tuition fees, booking flight tickets, or checking your bank balance, turn off Wi-Fi and use your mobile data. 
5. **Verify "Urgent" Admin Emails:** Phishing emails often try to scare you. If you get an email claiming your university ID will be blocked or your exam form is rejected unless you "click here to log in," don't panic. Check directly with your department or the IT desk first.
6. **Stop Sharing OTPs for "Quick Logins":** We casually share OTPs with friends to log into shared streaming accounts. Get out of this habit. Verify exactly what every OTP is for before reading it out loud, as scammers use this exact trick to hijack WhatsApp accounts.
7. **Say No to Screen-Sharing Apps:** If anyone calling you about a bank issue, refund, or KYC update asks you to download apps like AnyDesk, TeamViewer, or screenshare on a video call, hang up immediately. They are trying to watch you enter your passwords and OTPs.
8. **Lock Down Your Socials:** Keep your Instagram and Snapchat private. Scammers often look at public profiles to learn your daily routine, your friends' names, and your location to create highly believable fake emergency scenarios to trick your parents.

---

### 🚨 Emergency Helpline & Reporting Channels
If you realize you've been scammed, time is critical. Don't feel embarrassed—report it immediately to freeze the transaction:
* **Call the National Cyber Crime Helpline:** Dial **1930** (Available 24/7)
* **File a report online:** Visit the National Cyber Crime Portal at [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in)
