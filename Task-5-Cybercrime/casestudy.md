# 📄 Case Study: The Marketplace UPI Trap

**Cybercrime Type:** UPI / Online Payment Fraud 

UPI fraud often relies on social engineering rather than complex hacking. A prominent real-life variation targeting college students is the "Collect Request" scam, which exploits the rush of peer-to-peer online sales.

**The Incident (Step-by-Step):**
1. **The Bait:** Rajat, a second-year hostel student, listed his old bicycle on a local buying/selling app for ₹3,000. 
2. **Building Trust:** Within hours, a buyer contacted him, claiming to be a military officer newly posted to the city. The buyer agreed to the price without bargaining, creating a sense of urgency by saying he needed to lock in the deal before his shift ended.
3. **The "Test":** To build trust, the scammer successfully transferred ₹1 to Rajat’s Google Pay account. 
4. **The Trap:** The scammer then sent a "Collect Request" (a feature meant to ask for money) for the remaining ₹2,999. He called Rajat, instructing him to click 'Pay' and enter his UPI PIN, falsely explaining that "army merchant accounts" require the receiver to enter their PIN to accept the funds.
5. **Execution:** Eager to close the sale and trusting the "officer," Rajat entered his PIN. The money was instantly debited from his own account, and the scammer's number was immediately switched off.

**Targets and Consequences:**
Scammers specifically target college students selling second-hand goods (books, bikes, phones) because students are eager for quick cash and often rush through transactions. The immediate consequence is a painful financial loss, which hits hard on a limited student budget. Beyond the missing money, victims usually experience deep embarrassment, anxiety, and the stressful hassle of navigating bank disputes and cyber cell complaints.
